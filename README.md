# Banking-and-loan-lending-case-study
Lending institutions process millions of dollars daily, but without clear visibility into borrower demographics and portfolio health, they expose themselves to massive uncalculated risks.

**📌 Project Overview**

This project is a comprehensive data analytics case study of a consumer loan portfolio. Analyzing over 38,600 loan applications accounting for $436 Million in funded capital, this dashboard transforms raw financial data into a strategic narrative. It is designed to empower executive decision-making, optimize targeted marketing, and identify the root causes of charge-off risks.

🎯 Business Objectives
The primary goal of this analysis was to move beyond standard KPIs and answer critical business questions:

Portfolio Health: What is the ratio of good vs. bad loans, and are we profitable?

Borrower Persona: Who is our ideal customer, and why are they borrowing?

Risk Mitigation: Where is the business losing money, and how can we tighten underwriting?

💡 Key Insights & Strategic Recommendations
The $66M Leak: While the portfolio is generally healthy with an 86.2% Good Loan rate, the remaining 13.8% represents roughly $66M in funded bad loans.

Strategy: Conduct a deep-dive analysis into this specific 13.8% cohort to identify shared traits and tighten approval criteria.

The Myth of the "Fun" Loan: The overwhelming majority of capital is not being used for luxury purchases or vacations. Debt Consolidation is the dominant loan purpose, driving nearly half the total volume.

Strategy: Pivot marketing campaigns to specifically target consumers looking for a financial reset.

The Ideal Borrower Profile: The safest and most frequent applicants are highly stable individuals with 10+ years of employment, usually seeking a 36-month term. Furthermore, renters and mortgage holders make up an almost identical share of applications.

Strategy: Focus customer acquisition efforts on tenured professionals rather than relying solely on homeownership as a marker of stability.
